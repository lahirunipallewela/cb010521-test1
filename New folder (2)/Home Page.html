welcome to sri lanka

Sri Lanka, formerly Ceylon is an island within the Indian ocean, lies souths of India and separated from peninsular India by the Palk Strait. The country is additionally known around the world due to the impressive mountain tops,tea plantation and delightful temples.Sri Jayawardenapura Kotte is that the capital of Sri Lanka. The population of Sri Lanka is about twenty million. There are several communities in Sri Lanka . They're Sinhalese, Tamils, Muslims and the Burghers. The nationl anthem of Ceylon is 'Sri Lanka Matha'.The most common languages used in Sri Lanka are Sinhala, Tamil and English.

climate

Sri lanka has a topical cilmate and consists of disincs wet and sry seasons. Generally speaking,the costal area of Sri Lanka enjoy temperatures averaging 28oC while the upland areas are cooler and more temperate ,with a yearly average around 16-20oC.

culture

Sri Lanka is a land of great cultural diversity. Religion pervades many aspects of life and constitutes a basic element of this diversity. Buddhist temples, Hindu kovils also mosques, and churches, with their own colorful rituals, are the foremost readily visible features of the cultural landscape. Varying degrees of colonial impact, modernizing influences, and wealth and income add other shades to the cultural mosaic.

History

The history of Sri Lanka is intertwined with the history of the broader Indian subcontinent and the surrounding regions, comprising the areas of South Asia, Southeast Asia and Indian Ocean.The early human remains found on the island of Sri Lanka date to about 38,000 years ago (Balangoda Man).

Best time to visit Sri Lanka

  1. January - March (it's warm any sunny day for most of the island except trincomalee and Passekudah so it' s not the best time to be enjoying Sri Lanka's beaches here. )
  2. April(Hottest month of the year but the best times to be visiting the higher elevations of Kandy and the Tea Country with the evenings not so cool.)
  3. May - June (Heavy rains eveywhere in the island but beat tomr to visit Trincomalee Coast and Passekudah)
  4. July-September (inter-monsoon season you can still expect showers but also some glorious weather - all in all a great time to be exploring. )
  5. December(generally a great time to travel to Sri Lanka, with hot, dry weather following the end of the monsoon in the beach resorts of the south west coast. The north east is still feeling the effects of the Monsoon though so heading south is your best bet for time on the beach.)
