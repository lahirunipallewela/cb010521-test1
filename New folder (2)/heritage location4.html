The old town of Galle and its fortifications

The old town of Galle and its fortifications were founded in the 16th century by the Portuguese under the leadership of Lorenzo de Almedia and they made there first landing at the Galle Harbor. Galle reached the height of its development in the 18th century before the arrival of the British. Portuguese captured Gall from the Sihghalese King and build its first fortress naming it "santacruz". It is the best example of a fortified city built by Europeans in South and South-East Asia by showing the interaction between European architectural styles and South Asian traditions. Galle was first colonized by the Portuguese. In the early 17th century, the Dutch took over. They established the still existing fortress and ramparts. Also, the Dutch designed the look of the town.The old Town of Galle and fortifications is a UNESCO World heritage cultural site.

Location

It is situated in the southwest of Sri Lanka about 100 km south of Colombo, the city of Galle is on the shores of the Indian Ocean. It is constructed on a rocky peninsula which houses a natural port to which access is complicated by the presence of massive coral reefs.

Light house

Galle light house is one of the most poplar tourist attraction in galle , which was originally built by the Britishers.Galle Lighthouse is considered the oldest lighthouse of Sri Lanka. It is situated on the shores of galle. This lighthouse has been constructed as a replacement for the pre-existing lighthouse which was destroyed by fire in the year 1934. It is still in use as well as it serves a perfect tourist spot for the foreigners and locals alike
