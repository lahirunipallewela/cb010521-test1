The sacred city of Kandy

Kandy is the last Royal kindom of Sri lanka which is owned with a picturesque landscape surrounded by beautiful mountains which is 1600 feet above the mean sea level.Kandy well known for the Temple of the tooth relic and The Royal Botanical Gardens with more than 4000 labeled species of flora and also Kandy is a "Planned City" during British colonial period of ceylon.kandy was orginally called "senkadagalapura",then it was know as "nuwara" or "mahanuwara" which is the sinhala term for town or grand town.
During Augest Kandy town become more crowded due to the Kandy Esala Perehera which is annual tradition of the temple of the tooth. It usually attracts a large number of both foreign and local tourists. Your trip to kandy isn't completed without a tour around the charmin lake of kandy.By considering these reasons UNESCO declared kamdy as a world heritage under the name of Scared City of Kandy.

Points of interest



Temple of the tooth

The Temple of the tooth also known as "sri dalada maligawa" is one of the most beautiful and iconic buddhist temple in the country.It was bild in year 1595 during the regin of king Vimaladarmasuriyada first, at first it was the royal residence after that it was used to store the sacred tooth relic of load buddha and therefore is venerated by developees of the Buddhist faith from all corners of the world.

Peradeniya Royal Garden

Royal Botanical Garden is in peradeniya which is about 5.5km to the west of the kandy city in central province. It is near mahaweli River which is the longest river in sri lanka.It is also renowned for its collection of orchids.The garden includes more than 4000 species of plants including orchids, spices, medicinal plants and plam trees.The garden covers total area of 147 acres and its 460 meters above the sea level.it is managaed by the Division of National Botanical Gardens of the Department of Agriculture.the botanical garden was formed by Alexander Moon in 1821, he used it for grow coffee and cinnamon plants.
The garden is a home to a large and diverse variety of plants especially including endemics and species extinct in the wild.These gardens receive considerable rainfall both during the South West and North East monsoons and hence are lush and verdant throughout the year. Three seasons are earmarked as April to June, August to September and December which is when most visitors fill the gardens and flowering is at its peak.

kandy lake

kandy lake also known as sea of milk or kiri muhuda in sinhala is an artifical lake in the heart of kandy. It was build in 1807 by King Sri Wickrama Rajasinghe next to the temple of tooth, as the time passes by it was reduced in size. It is a protected lake with fishing banned.as you travel around the lake you can see the Diyathilika Mandapaya, Walakula Bamma anmd Ulpange.
Diyathilika Mandapaya is a dam for purpose of reaching the oppodite sude if the lake.The King Sri Wickrama Rajasingha removed the soil from both the palace end and the Malwaththa Viharaya end , leaving a island behind.At first it was used as a summeer house for the Rooyals but in the later the British used it as an ammunition store and added fortress style parapet aroumd its perimeter.
Walakula Bamma also known as clouds wall is the wall that surround the lake.It was build to increase the beauty of the lake.
the Ulpange is situated partly in the waters of the kandy lake and it was used as queens bathing pavilon but today it is used as police post.
