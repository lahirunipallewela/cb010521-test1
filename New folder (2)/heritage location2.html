The ancient city of Sigiriya

Sigiriya,also named as Lion Rock or Lion Mountain ,site in central sri lanka . The ruins of the capital built by the parricidal King Kassapa durring the regin of 473-495 which is slocated in the northen Matale distric near the town of dabulla in central province of Sri lanka .It is named as one of the most famous Archeoloical Treasure and UNESCO named sigiriya rock as world heritage site in 1982 under the name of "Ancient City of Sigiriya Sri Lanka".

Architecture

The fortified garden city of Sigiiya rock fortress is an exceptional master piece of ancient urban planning and architecture.

Lion Rock Sigiriya Graffiti

one of the main features of Sigiriya rock is Sigiri graffiti.They were written on the surface of the Mirror Wall at Sigiriya.There are are over 1800 pieces of prose, poetry and commentary written by ancient tourists on the wall. These graffiti offer a fascinating insight into the history of Sigiriya and the evolution of language in Sri Lanka over a period of 800 years. The majority of the graffiti refer to the beautiful paintings of semi-nude females. These graffiti confirm about residence of King Kashyapa who ruled from 477 to 495 AD.
Many poems are written in a Sinhala, Sanskrit and Tamil. These early scribblers have left us priceless insights into the past. Most of the proplr in the paintings has been identified accourding to ther socail rank , name and from the town they came from..

Lion Staircase

The lion staircase is situated half way up the sigiriya rock,it is an impressive cottage which protects the entry yo the sky Palace.It is build in a shape of crouching lion,we have to enter through its charset.The height of the gatehouse is 35meters while the width is 21 meters wide and protruded 11meters from the rock face.
The paws of the lion and staircase are all that remain now.The paws and the nails of the lion is as tall as a fully grown man. the brightly colored , eyes blazed, and its mouth ageape it appeared ready to swallow anyone who dared to approach it.

Large pond

To the southest of sigirya you can find a large pond measuring 27x 21 meters. The western side of this pond is hewn from the rock and the other three sides built up with stone slabs and bricks. The walls have been plastered.They have grew aquatic plants which flowered in various hues throughout the year. A number of stairways within this pond area. For example, one steep staircase merely leads to a landing up one side of the pond, possibly a resting spot for a swimmer.
